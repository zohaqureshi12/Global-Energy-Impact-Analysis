# Global Energy & Economic Impact Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to investigate the relationship between a nation's economic wealth (GDP) and its energy consumption patterns. As the world shifts towards sustainability, this analysis specifically compares the industrial journeys and energy transitions of India, China, and the USA.

The aim of this project is to explore how economic growth affects energy consumption and emissions. By analyzing real-world data, the notebook highlights how India, China, and the USA have evolved in terms of energy usage, efficiency, and renewable adoption.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- **Python** – Core programming language  
- **Pandas** – Data cleaning and manipulation  
- **NumPy** – Numerical computations and feature engineering  
- **Matplotlib** – Data visualization and trend analysis  
- **Jupyter Notebook** – Interactive analysis environment

## 🚀 Key Technical Features
Data Cleaning & Wrangling: Processed a sparse global dataset, specifically filtering for modern records (1990–2018) to ensure analytical accuracy and remove historical noise.

Feature Engineering: Created custom metrics using NumPy and Pandas .loc logic:

Energy per Capita: Normalized energy usage by population to allow fair comparison between countries.

Renewable Share: Calculated the percentage of total energy derived from solar and wind sources.

Energy Intensity: Measured how much energy is required to produce one unit of GDP (efficiency tracking).

Aggregate Analysis: Performed decade-level grouping to identify structural shifts in energy demand across 10-year blocks.

Statistical Insights: Conducted correlation analysis to quantify the link between economic growth (GDP) and CO2 emissions.

## 📊 Visual Analysis Summary
The notebook includes several high-impact visualizations:

Energy Consumption Trends: A multi-line chart tracking the rise of India and China against the stable usage of the USA.

Renewable Energy Share: A focused analysis of how green energy is becoming a larger part of the national energy mix.

Decade-Based Comparison: Grouped bar charts showing average energy growth per decade.

Per Capita Comparisons: Identifying the industrial gap and efficiency trends between developed and emerging nations.
